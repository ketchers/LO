Explanation


To find the expected counts multiply the total number of observations by the expected probability for an outcome assuming independence. This is given by $$E_{i,j} = \frac{(\text{sum of row }i)\cdot (\text{sum of column }j)}{N}.$$ The expected counts are shown in the following table:

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{red}&\textbf{blue}&\textbf{green}&\textbf{orange}&\textbf{Total}\\ \hline \textbf{140 - 160} & 7.15&10.7&11&12.1&41\\ \hline \textbf{160 - 180} & 10.5&15.7&16.1&17.7&60\\ \hline \textbf{180 - 200} & 8.38&12.6&12.9&14.2&48\\ \hline \textbf{Total} & 26&39&40&44&149\\ \hline \end{array}Recall that the observed counts are:

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{red}&\textbf{blue}&\textbf{green}&\textbf{orange}&\textbf{Total}\\ \hline \textbf{140 - 160} & \text{5}&\text{15}&\text{9}&\text{12}&\text{41}\\ \hline \textbf{160 - 180} & \text{12}&\text{15}&\text{18}&\text{15}&\text{60}\\ \hline \textbf{180 - 200} & \text{9}&\text{9}&\text{13}&\text{17}&\text{48}\\ \hline \textbf{Total} & \text{26}&\text{39}&\text{40}&\text{44}&\text{149}\\ \hline \end{array}

The degrees of freedom are $_df = (4 - 1)(3 - 1) = 6$_ and the $_\chi^2$_-statistic is:$$\chi^2_{6}=\sum_{i=1}^{3}\sum_{j=1}^{4} \frac{(O_{i,j}-E_{i,j})^2}{E_{i,j}} = 5.23$$

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{Strongly Agree}&\textbf{Agree}&\textbf{Nuetral}&\textbf{Disagree}&\textbf{Strongly Disagree}&\textbf{Total}\\ \hline \textbf{Men} & \text{9}&\text{14}&\text{7}&\text{5}&\text{5}&\text{40}\\ \hline \textbf{Women} & \text{12}&\text{7}&\text{13}&\text{6}&\text{7}&\text{45}\\ \hline \textbf{Total} & \text{21}&\text{21}&\text{20}&\text{11}&\text{12}&\text{85}\\ \hline \end{array}

The degrees of freedom are $_df = (5 - 1)(2 - 1) = 4$_ and the $_\chi^2$_-statistic is:$$\chi^2_{4}=\sum_{i=1}^{2}\sum_{j=1}^{5} \frac{(O_{i,j}-E_{i,j})^2}{E_{i,j}} = 4.71$$

The p-value for this data is: $$\text{p-value} = P(\chi^2_{4} > 4.71) = 31.86\%$$

The lightly shaded histogram represents a sampling distribution of 5000 sample $_\chi^2$_-statistics where the samples are of the same size as the given observed data (85 observations). This indicate how well the $_\chi^2$_-distributions is capturing the actual sampling distribution for this experiment.

The p-value is greater than the $_\alpha$_-level so the null hypothesis is not rejected. More precisely, assuming the null hypothesis: H0: colors and weight are independent. there is a 31.86% probability due to random chance in sampling that the difference in the expected and observed data is at least this large.
