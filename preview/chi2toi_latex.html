Question


A group of adults was asked about their preference in colors. The participants were put into categories by weight. Test at the level $_\alpha = 0.01$_ whether colors and weight are independent.
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{red}&\textbf{blue}&\textbf{green}&\textbf{orange}\\ \hline \textbf{140 - 160} & \text{9}&\text{10}&\text{7}&\text{11}\\ \hline \textbf{160 - 180} & \text{8}&\text{10}&\text{12}&\text{5}\\ \hline \textbf{180 - 200} & \text{18}&\text{6}&\text{5}&\text{25}\\ \hline \end{array} Compute the $_\chi^2$_-statistic and degrees of freedom for the given observations.

Choices


\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{Choices}\\ \hline \textbf{Answer} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 6 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 19}\\ \hline \textbf{Distractor} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 12 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 25.2}\\ \hline \textbf{Distractor} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 12 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 19}\\ \hline \textbf{Distractor} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 6 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 4.05}\\ \hline \textbf{Distractor} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 6 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 25.2}\\ \hline \end{array}

Explanation


To find the expected counts multiply the total number of observations by the expected probability for an outcome assuming independence. This is given by $$E_{i,j} = \frac{(\text{sum of row }i)\cdot (\text{sum of column }j)}{N}.$$ The expected counts are shown in the following table:

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{red}&\textbf{blue}&\textbf{green}&\textbf{orange}&\textbf{Total}\\ \hline \textbf{140 - 160} & 10.3&7.63&7.05&12&37\\ \hline \textbf{160 - 180} & 9.72&7.22&6.67&11.4&35\\ \hline \textbf{180 - 200} & 15&11.1&10.3&17.6&54\\ \hline \textbf{Total} & 35&26&24&41&126\\ \hline \end{array}Recall that the observed counts are:

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{red}&\textbf{blue}&\textbf{green}&\textbf{orange}&\textbf{Total}\\ \hline \textbf{140 - 160} & \text{9}&\text{10}&\text{7}&\text{11}&\text{37}\\ \hline \textbf{160 - 180} & \text{8}&\text{10}&\text{12}&\text{5}&\text{35}\\ \hline \textbf{180 - 200} & \text{18}&\text{6}&\text{5}&\text{25}&\text{54}\\ \hline \textbf{Total} & \text{35}&\text{26}&\text{24}&\text{41}&\text{126}\\ \hline \end{array}
The degrees of freedom are $_df = (4 - 1)(3 - 1) = 6$_ and the $_\chi^2$_-statistic is:$$\chi^2_{6}=\sum_{i=1}^{3}\sum_{j=1}^{4} \frac{(O_{i,j}-E_{i,j})^2}{E_{i,j}} = 19$$
