Question


A group of adults was asked about their preference in colors. The participants were put into categories by age. Test at the level $_\alpha = 0.01$_ whether colors and age are independent.

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{red}&\textbf{blue}&\textbf{green}&\textbf{orange}\\ \hline \textbf{25 - 31} & \text{7}&\text{14}&\text{12}&\text{17}\\ \hline \textbf{32 - 38} & \text{14}&\text{21}&\text{5}&\text{5}\\ \hline \textbf{38 - 44} & \text{7}&\text{13}&\text{9}&\text{11}\\ \hline \end{array} Compute the $_\chi^2$_-statistic and degrees of freedom for the given observations.

Choices


Answer
(degrees of freedom) df = 6 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 13.7
Distractor
(degrees of freedom) df = 6 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 3.71

Distractor

Explanation


To find the expected counts multiply the total number of observations by the expected probability for an outcome assuming independence. This is given by $$E_{i,j} = \frac{(\text{sum of row }i)\cdot (\text{sum of column }j)}{N}.$$ The expected counts are shown in the following table:

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{red}&\textbf{blue}&\textbf{green}&\textbf{orange}&\textbf{Total}\\ \hline \textbf{25 - 31} & 10.4&17.8&9.63&12.2&50\\ \hline \textbf{32 - 38} & 9.33&16&8.67&11&45\\ \hline \textbf{38 - 44} & 8.3&14.2&7.7&9.78&40\\ \hline \textbf{Total} & 28&48&26&33&135\\ \hline \end{array}Recall that the observed counts are:

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{red}&\textbf{blue}&\textbf{green}&\textbf{orange}&\textbf{Total}\\ \hline \textbf{25 - 31} & \text{7}&\text{14}&\text{12}&\text{17}&\text{50}\\ \hline \textbf{32 - 38} & \text{14}&\text{21}&\text{5}&\text{5}&\text{45}\\ \hline \textbf{38 - 44} & \text{7}&\text{13}&\text{9}&\text{11}&\text{40}\\ \hline \textbf{Total} & \text{28}&\text{48}&\text{26}&\text{33}&\text{135}\\ \hline \end{array}

The degrees of freedom are $_df = (4 - 1)(3 - 1) = 6$_ and the $_\chi^2$_-statistic is:$$\chi^2_{6}=\sum_{i=1}^{3}\sum_{j=1}^{4} \frac{(O_{i,j}-E_{i,j})^2}{E_{i,j}} = 13.7$$
