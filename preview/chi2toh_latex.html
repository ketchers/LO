Question


A group of adults was asked about their preference in foods. The participants were put into categories by weight. Test at the level $_\alpha = 0.01$_ whether foods and weight are independent.
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{pizza}&\textbf{burger}&\textbf{hotdog}&\textbf{sushi}\\ \hline \textbf{140 - 160} & \text{12}&\text{11}&\text{12}&\text{8}\\ \hline \textbf{160 - 180} & \text{7}&\text{8}&\text{8}&\text{17}\\ \hline \textbf{180 - 200} & \text{14}&\text{12}&\text{15}&\text{13}\\ \hline \end{array} Compute the $_\chi^2$_-statistic and degrees of freedom for the given observations.

Choices


\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{Answer}&\textbf{Distractor}&\textbf{Distractor}&\textbf{Distractor}&\textbf{Distractor}\\ \hline \textbf{C} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 6 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 6.73}\\ \hline \textbf{h} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 6 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 2.59}\\ \hline \textbf{o} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 12 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 2.16}\\ \hline \textbf{i} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 6 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 2.16}\\ \hline \textbf{c} & \text{(degrees of freedom) df = 12 and the $_\chi^2$_-test statistic = 6.65}\\ \hline \end{array}

Explanation


To find the expected counts multiply the total number of observations by the expected probability for an outcome assuming independence. This is given by $$E_{i,j} = \frac{(\text{sum of row }i)\cdot (\text{sum of column }j)}{N}.$$ The expected counts are shown in the following table:

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{pizza}&\textbf{burger}&\textbf{hotdog}&\textbf{sushi}&\textbf{Total}\\ \hline \textbf{140 - 160} & 10.4&9.73&11&11.9&43\\ \hline \textbf{160 - 180} & 9.64&9.05&10.2&11.1&40\\ \hline \textbf{180 - 200} & 13&12.2&13.8&15&54\\ \hline \textbf{Total} & 33&31&35&38&137\\ \hline \end{array}Recall that the observed counts are:

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \textbf{}&\textbf{pizza}&\textbf{burger}&\textbf{hotdog}&\textbf{sushi}&\textbf{Total}\\ \hline \textbf{140 - 160} & \text{12}&\text{11}&\text{12}&\text{8}&\text{43}\\ \hline \textbf{160 - 180} & \text{7}&\text{8}&\text{8}&\text{17}&\text{40}\\ \hline \textbf{180 - 200} & \text{14}&\text{12}&\text{15}&\text{13}&\text{54}\\ \hline \textbf{Total} & \text{33}&\text{31}&\text{35}&\text{38}&\text{137}\\ \hline \end{array}
The degrees of freedom are $_df = (4 - 1)(3 - 1) = 6$_ and the $_\chi^2$_-statistic is:$$\chi^2_{6}=\sum_{i=1}^{3}\sum_{j=1}^{4} \frac{(O_{i,j}-E_{i,j})^2}{E_{i,j}} = 6.73$$
