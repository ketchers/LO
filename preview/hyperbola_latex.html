Explanation


The first step to finding the graph of $$- x^{2} - 6 x + 4 y^{2} - 32 y + 51 = 0$$ is to find the normal form of the equation. To begin move the constant term to the right hand side. This gives: $$(- x^{2} - 6 x) + (4 y^{2} - 32 y) = -51$$Next factor common factors from the $_x$_ and $_y$_ terms to get: $$4(y^{2} - 8 y) - (x^{2} + 6 x) = -51$$Now complete the squares: $$4\left(y^{2} - 8 y + \left(\frac{-8}{2}\right)^2\right) - \left(x^{2} + 6 x + \left(\frac{6}{2}\right)^2\right) = -51 + 4\left(\frac{-8}{2}\right)^2 - 1\left(\frac{6}{2}\right)^2$$This simplifies to: $$4\left(y - 4\right)^2 - \left(x + 3\right)^2 = 4$$Lastly divide both side by the right hand side to get: $$\left(y - 4\right)^2 - \frac{1}{4}\left(x + 3\right)^2 = 1$$This simplifies to the final normal form: $$\frac{(y - 4)^2}{1^2} - \frac{(x + 3)^2}{2^2} = 1$$From this we can read off the center to be at $_(h,k) = (-3, 4)$_. The tansverse (major) axis is along $_x = 4$_ and has length $_2a = 2$_. The vertices are $_(h, - a + k) = (-3,3)$_ and $_(h, a + k) = (-3, 5)$_. The conjugate (minor) axis is along $_x = -3$_ and has length $_2b = 4$_. The co-vertices are $_(h - 2, k) = (-5,4)$_ and $_(h + 2, k) = (-1, 4)$_. The two assymptotes are $_y = \pm\frac{b}{a}(x + 3) + 4 = \pm\frac{2}{1}(x + 3) + 4 $_. Finally, the focal length is $_c = \sqrt{a^2+b^2}=\sqrt{5}$_ and the foci are located at $_(h, - c + k) = (-3,- \sqrt{5} + 4)$_ and $_(h, c + k) = (-3, \sqrt{5} + 4)$_.

Select the correct graph of the hyperbola given by the equations: $$x^{2} - \frac{\left(y - 3\right)^{2}}{4} = 1$$

Explanation


The hyperbola is given in standard normal form: $$\frac{(x)^2}{1^2} - \frac{(y - 3)^2}{2^2} = 1$$From this we can read off the center to be at $_(h,k) = (0, 3)$_. The tansverse (major) axis is along $_y = 3$_ and has length $_2a = 2$_. The vertices are $_(- a + h, k) = (-1,3)$_ and $_(a + h, k) = (1, 3)$_. The conjugate (minor) axis is along $_y = 0$_ and has length $_2b = 4$_. The co-vertices are $_(h, k - 2) = (0,1)$_ and $_(h, k + 2) = (0, 5)$_. The two assymptotes are $_y = \pm\frac{b}{a}(x) + 3 = \pm\frac{2}{1}(x) + 3 $_. Finally, the focal length is $_c = \sqrt{a^2+b^2}=\sqrt{5}$_ and the foci are located at $_(- c + h, k) = (- \sqrt{5},3)$_ and $_(c + h, k) = (\sqrt{5}, 3)$_.
\begin{array}{|c|c|}\hline \text{center}&\text{$_(0, 3)$_}\\ \hline \text{vertices}&\text{$_(-1, 3), (1, 3)$_}\\ \hline \text{length of conjugate axis}&\text{$_2$_}\\ \hline \text{co-vertices}&\text{$_(0, 1), (0, 1)$_}\\ \hline \text{length of conjugate axis}&\text{$_4$_}\\ \hline \text{foci}&\text{$_(- \sqrt{5}, 3), (\sqrt{5}, 3)$_}\\ \hline \text{asymptotes}&\text{$_y = \pm\frac{2}{1}(x) + 3$_}\\ \hline \end{array}
